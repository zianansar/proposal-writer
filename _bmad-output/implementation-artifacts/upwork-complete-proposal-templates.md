## Proposal Writing Workflow

**Step 1: Job Analysis (3 minutes)**
- Read job post completely twice
- Identify job category
- Extract client name (if available)
- Note 2-3 specific project details to reference
- Check for special instructions or screening questions
- Research client's business/previous hires if time permits

**Step 2: Template Selection (1 minute)**
- Choose template matching job type
- Or use General Template Structure

**Step 3: Customization (10 minutes)**
- Select appropriate hook formula
- Customize solution framework with job-specific details
- Choose most relevant portfolio sample
- Write specific, valuable CTA
- Replace ALL [bracketed variables]
- Ensure client name and specific details appear in first paragraph

**Step 4: Quality Check (3 minutes)**
- Word count: 150-250 words? ✓
- All [brackets] replaced? ✓
- Client-specific details in first 3 sentences? ✓
- No generic/template language? ✓
- Grammar and spelling check ✓
- All job post instructions followed? ✓
- Mobile-readable formatting? ✓

**Step 5: Final Polish (2 minutes)**
- Read aloud to check flow
- Verify links work
- Confirm tone matches client's job post
- One final proofread

**Total Time: ~20 minutes per proposal**

## Master Customization Checklist

**Before sending ANY proposal, verify:**

**Personalization (Critical):**
- [ ] Client's name used (if findable)
- [ ] 2+ specific details from job post referenced
- [ ] Industry/business context mentioned
- [ ] Tone matches their writing style (formal/casual)

**Opening (Make or Break):**
- [ ] First 2-3 sentences are compelling and unique
- [ ] No generic opens ("Dear Sir/Madam", "I am a developer")
- [ ] Passes 5-second test (would YOU click to read more?)

**Solution (The Core):**
- [ ] 3-4 specific, actionable steps
- [ ] Mentions technologies/approaches from job post
- [ ] Includes realistic timeline
- [ ] Focuses on THEIR outcomes, not your skills

**Proof (Credibility):**
- [ ] 1-2 relevant work samples included
- [ ] Links are working and relevant
- [ ] At least one quantified result
- [ ] Recent work (ideally last 6 months)

**CTA (Next Step):**
- [ ] Clear, specific action requested
- [ ] Low-friction (10-min call, not "hire me")
- [ ] Includes availability or follow-up plan

**Technical (Must-Haves):**
- [ ] 150-250 words total
- [ ] No spelling or grammar errors
- [ ] Proper formatting (bullets, paragraphs, white space)
- [ ] All special instructions from job post followed
- [ ] Mobile-readable (short paragraphs, clear structure)

**Final Check:**
- [ ] Could I send this exact proposal to 3+ jobs? (If yes, TOO GENERIC)
- [ ] Does it sound like a human wrote it for this specific client?
- [ ] Would I hire me based on this proposal?

## Common Mistakes Checklist (What NOT to Do)

**Avoid These Red Flags:**
- ❌ Starting with "Dear Hiring Manager" or "To Whom It May Concern"
- ❌ "I am confident I can help you" (generic claim)
- ❌ "Please review my profile/portfolio" (lazy)
- ❌ Mentioning skills they didn't ask for
- ❌ Proposals over 300 words
- ❌ Proposals under 100 words
- ❌ Copy-pasting from previous proposals
- ❌ Using all caps or excessive exclamation marks!!!
- ❌ Overpromising unrealistic results
- ❌ Not following special instructions in job post
- ❌ Relevant portfolio samples missing
- ❌ No clear call to action
- ❌ Desperate tone ("I really need this job")
- ❌ Pricing discussion before demonstrating value
- ❌ More than 2 attachments
